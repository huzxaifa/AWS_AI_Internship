Potential Sources of bias across systems

Biased training data
Historical data reflects past human discrimination (e.g., against certain races, genders, neighborhoods), so models learn and reproduce those patterns.

Unbalanced representation
Some groups appear much less in the dataset (e.g., darker‑skinned faces, women, people with disabilities), so the system is less accurate for them.

Proxy features for protected traits
Variables like postcode, school, club membership, tenure gaps, or non‑English names act as stand‑ins for race, class, or gender, even if those traits are never explicitly used.

Sagemaker Clarify features and capabilities

Bias Detection: Analyzes data and models for biases (e.g., gender or age bias) throughout the ML lifecycle—pre-training, post-training, and with live endpoint monitoring.

Model Explainability: Uses feature importance metrics (e.g., SHAP values) to explain how different input features influence model predictions.

Monitoring: Integrates with SageMaker Model Monitor to detect bias drift and feature importance drift in production.

Generative AI Evaluation: Evaluates Foundation Models (FMs) for accuracy, toxicity, and robustness, offering both automatic and human-in-the-loop assessments.

Reports: Generates detailed reports to assist in understanding, debugging, and demonstrating model fairness to stakeholder
